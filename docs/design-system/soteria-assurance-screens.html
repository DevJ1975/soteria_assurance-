§ 6.1.2
Hazard Identification & Assessment of OH&S Risks
Clause 6 Planning HIRA Risk Assessment
What the Standard Requires

Clause 6.1.2 requires the organization to establish, implement and maintain a process for hazard identification that is ongoing and proactive. It is the engine of the OH&S management system — if hazards are not reliably surfaced, every downstream control is built on incomplete information.

ISO REQUIREMENT
The process shall take into account routine and non-routine activities; human factors; new or changed hazards; potential emergency situations; and how work is actually organized — including the people in the workplace and those affected by its activities. § 6.1.2.1 (a–f)
What to Audit
  • Whether the HIRA methodology is documented, repeatable and risk-based.
  • Coverage of routine, non-routine and emergency scenarios across all areas in scope.
  • Evidence that workers were consulted in identifying hazards (links to §5.4).
  • Whether recent incidents and changes have been fed back into the register.
Auditor Tip
Sample by activity, not by document. Pick a high-risk task observed on the floor and trace it back to the register — gaps surface faster than a top-down document review.
Sample Interview Questions
Q1Walk me through how a new hazard gets added to the register.
Q2How are non-routine tasks — maintenance, shutdowns — assessed?
Q3When were workers last involved in a hazard identification review?
Q4Show me where the last incident changed a risk score.
Typical Documents to Review
HIRA register / risk assessments
Documented HIRA methodology
Worker consultation records
Incident & near-miss reports
Common Nonconformities
Most Frequently Raised
• Register treated as a one-time document, not an ongoing process.
• Non-routine and emergency tasks omitted entirely.
• No demonstrable worker participation in hazard identification.
• Methodology undocumented — scores applied inconsistently between areas.
